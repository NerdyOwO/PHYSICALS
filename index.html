Overview

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is strongly linked to the worsening of melasma, making photoprotection a central strategy in managing the condition. Photoprotection spans a range of interventions, from topical sunscreens and physical barriers to oral antioxidants and lifestyle changes.

Evidence from clinical studies supports this approach. For example, research in India showed that patients with melasma who used a broad-spectrum sunscreen (SPF 19, PA+++) for 12 weeks experienced significant improvements.

Ultimately, robust photoprotection tailored to melasma not only prevents flare-ups but also empowers patients with practical tools to cope with UV-related challenges, improving both treatment results and overall quality of life.

Proper Usage

1) Apply 15–20 minutes before sun exposure for full absorption.

2) Follow the 2-finger rule: two strips of sunscreen on index and middle finger, apply evenly.

3) Don’t forget often-missed areas: ears, neck, and hands.

4) Reapply every 2 hours or after swimming/sweating/towel-drying.

5) Apply as final skincare step in the morning before or after makeup.

6) Daily use is crucial—apply even on cloudy days or indoors (UV penetrates glass).
